New Kawaii Draft League Rules

Rules

  • The New Kawaii Draft League is a fun, casual league where players earn points for match wins during Friday night drafts.
  • League seasons are structured around each set release.
  • Each season commences with the first Friday night draft following the prerelease weekend and concludes on the Friday preceding the prerelease of the subsequent set.
  • The season culminates in a draft featuring the top eight players present on the final Friday. The size of the final table may be adjusted according to total attendance to avoid disrupting regular draft activities.
    For example, if thirteen players are present, the tables may be divided into groups of seven and six players.
  • The winner of the final draft is designated as the season champion.
  • Current season standings can be viewed on the Leaderboard page.

Points

  • Players will be awarded 3 points per match win and 1 point per match draw.
  • Players will be ranked based on total points. The first tiebreaker will be wins, followed by matches played.

Ratings

  • Any participant in a league event will be assigned an Elo rating, a predictive rating system used to represent a playerâ€™s relative skill level. Lifetime Elo ratings are published on the Lifetime Ratings page.
  • All players begin with a base Elo rating of 1600. When competing against opponents with similar Elo ratings, a player will typically gain 8 Elo points for a win and lose 8 Elo points for a loss.
  • For higher Rules Enforcement Level (REL) competitive events, such as Regional Championship Qualifiers (RCQs), Elo point adjustments will be doubled relative to standard draft events.
  • Each event page includes a section highlighting notable upsets, defined as matches in which the winning player gains 10 or more Elo points.
